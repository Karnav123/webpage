I am pursuing Master of Technology (2018-21) in the Department of Systems and Control Engineering at IIT Bombay. My Master program is being funded by FOSSEE, where I have been working as a research assistant under the supervision of Prof. Kannan Moudgalya. The FOSSEE project is part of the National Mission on Education through Information and Communication Technology (ICT), Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD), Government of India.

I am 24 years (and counting) tricenarian, who has spent more than 10 years of his life in different hostels. It includes a major chunk spent at JNV Muzaffarpur , where I started my school. Here, I learned the most important lesson of life that one should never buy a bucket while staying in hostels. I also learned the importance of grades here, as I was made the class captain when I secured the first rank in my class. After realizing the power of a class captain, I tried my best to maintain my performance, which luckily used to happen. Apart from this, my latent passion for eloquence was unleashed here. Henceforth, I participated in literary events and visited places due to this association.

Another chunk of my hostel life can be traced back in Shri Mata Vaishno Devi University Katra, Jammu & Kashmir, where I completed my Bachelor of Technology. Though I was blessed to get in one of the most beautiful campuses in India, I had to learn certain maneuvers to survive extreme weather conditions there. Also, I was under tremendous pressure to prove my decision of pursuing BTech; since I had failed IIT-JEE to get admitted in this college. Thus, the life of undergrad college made me learn n number of lessons, which I owe to my professors and the ambiance of the place.

I have been to three different nations (including Nepal). In India, I have traveled to more than 10 states including the extreme points Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Assam etc. Maybe, due to my passion for traveling and exploring places, I changed places from NTHU Taiwan to IIT Mandi to IIT Bombay in the duration from December 2016 to May 2017. Even today, I love traveling, however, I don’t post my check-ins on social media, despite the fact that I use Airtel 4G. According to Google Maps Timeline, I have covered 28% of the way around the world (obviously not on foot) in 2018.

Due to my upbringing in Bihar, I am quite active in keeping track of political events across the world. I love analyzing the government policies and its impact on the masses. I might miss some of the cricket matches, but I can’t afford to miss the election results. However, I don’t take these results to my heart, as I enjoy being a proud Indian. I love writing blogs apart from technical papers. Some of the Blogs I wrote to grab just a T-shirt from Internshala. However, I was surprised to know that these Blogs have helped a few students as well. Recently, one of my Blogs was featured in The Hindu. These days, I am also trying my hands at photography to keep my Instagram account alive. In my leisure time, I enjoy cooking and playing badminton. However, I am more efficient in the former than the latter one.

Overall, I am hale and hearty and do whatever I love (or love whatever I do).
